Hi, Basecamp!

My name is Sarah. I like exploring new places, learning new things, and making beautiful, usable websites.

I think we should work together. Here’s why.

My pen is mightier than my sword.

I always wanted to be a writer when I grew up. Before I switched to design, I studied literary arts and journalism. (If you need a glosa written, I'm your girl.) These days, I help my clients with copyediting and maintain a rather verbose blog. I believe that words and design are intricately linked: neither is effective alone. Great design cannot exist without sharp, well-written copy. Good grammar means a lot to me. You'll never catch me designing a page that encourages users to "signup for this service."

I eat (your own) dog food.

I've been using Basecamp every day for the past five years. It's a vital part of my daily routine. With 37Signals' focus shifting to all-Basecamp, all the time, you're going to want someone on board who's intimately familiar with the product. That's me. I love Basecamp, and I want to help make it better. (I'd love it if Basecamp had the ability to request a follow-up on an item.) I'd love to work on a product I use every single day.

Office not required.

From Colombia, to Croatia, to Cambodia: I've worked everywhere. For the last few years, I've travelled extensively, all while maintaining a profitable, thriving freelance business. As a result, I have great time management skills, I communicate effectively, and I have no problems keeping myself motivated. I've had Skype meetings at midnight and at 7am. I've coordinated with team members from all over the globe. I make things happen. I don't believe that cubicles foster creativity, and they certainly don't make for healthy work environments.

Made with love.

I care about doing good work. I care about my clients. I care about the people who use the things I make. Caring means you invest your mental energies into making something great. I like being able to afford Louboutins and Cristal, but ultimately, that's not what motivates me. I want to make beautiful, usable things that people love. Basecamp has hundreds of thousands of passionate, opinionated users who care a great deal, I'm one of them. I want to help make it even better.

I believe in transparency.

This is why you can find me on my business' blog, talking about nude modelling or run-ins with the Thai police. I believe that businesses should be responsible to their customers, and I've applied this to my own business. Basecamp's policy of transparency makes me happy. And your world-class customer support is something I'd like to get behind.

Oh, and I'm a pretty good designer, too.

There's no way my business would be celebrating its eighth birthday and sending me on round-the-world trips if I weren't. I'm great with HTML and CSS, and I've got a pretty solid familiarity with Rails and Javascript. I've done some work on iOS and web apps. I pick up new skills quickly, and I'm always trying to learn new things and better my process.

This is the primary reason why I want to work at Basecamp. I love running my business, and the decision to transition away from it isn't one I take lightly. I am looking for work that will fullfil and challenge me. I want to work with passionate people who challenge me to grow as a designer. I believe Basecamp is this company.

Let's make great things together.
